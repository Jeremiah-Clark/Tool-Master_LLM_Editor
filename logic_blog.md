# Blog Writing Logic Module

*Engagement, topic focus, audience reach, and scanning behavior.*

------

## 1. Topic & Audience Clarity

- **Single Focus:** Each blog post should address one primary topic or question. If you're covering multiple angles, use subheadings to compartmentalize.
- **Audience First:** Define who you're writing for (skill level, background, what they care about). This shapes everything—tone, depth, examples, jargon.
- **The Payoff:** Make clear in the opening what the reader will learn or be able to do after reading. Don't bury the value proposition.

## 2. Hook & Discoverability

- **Headline Clarity:** The headline should be specific, benefit-driven, and scannable. Avoid vague or cute headlines that don't signal the content.
- **Opening Hook:** The first 1–2 sentences should grab attention or promise value. Follow with a clear "why you should read this."
- **Keyword Awareness:** If this post will be shared or discovered via search, naturally incorporate 2–3 primary keywords in the title, opening, and headings.

## 3. Scanning & Skimmability

- **Short Paragraphs:** Aim for 2–4 sentences per paragraph. Walls of text lose readers.
- **Generous Headings:** Use H2 and H3 subheadings liberally to break up content and guide scanning.
- **Lists Over Prose:** When explaining multiple points or steps, use bullet lists or numbered lists instead of dense paragraphs.
- **White Space:** Embrace it. More breathing room = higher perceived readability.

## 4. Examples & Concrete Details

- **Worked Examples:** Abstract concepts are forgettable. Follow theoretical explanations with a specific, relatable example or scenario.
- **Specificity Over Vagueness:** Say "a 500-word blog post published on Tuesday" instead of "a piece published sometime."
- **Real Scenarios:** If possible, use real-world or realistic examples rather than purely hypothetical ones.

## 5. Call-to-Action or Payoff

- **Closing Clarity:** End the post by reinforcing what the reader learned or what they can do next. Don't just stop abruptly.
- **Optional CTA:** Depending on your goals, include a next step (subscribe, download, try this, read more). Keep it brief and relevant.
- **Avoid Soft Endings:** Don't fade out with "and that's why this matters"—be direct about the takeaway.

## 6. Link & Reference Strategy

- **Hyperlink Sparingly:** 2–4 outbound links per post is typical. Link to high-authority or highly relevant sources.
- **Link Anchor Text:** Use 2–4 words that describe where the link goes (e.g., "read this case study" instead of "click here").
- **Internal Links:** If you have a body of work, link back to related posts to increase engagement and SEO.

## 7. Tone & Voice Consistency

- **Conversational But Credible:** Adopt a tone that feels natural—neither robotic nor off-the-cuff.
- **Avoid Tone Whiplash:** If you open with dry expertise, don't suddenly shift to overly casual or sarcastic mid-post.
- **Personality Without Distraction:** Let your voice show, but don't let it overshadow the content.

## 8. Platform Awareness

- **Share-Friendly:** Structure your post so it works well when shared on social media (headline makes sense in isolation, key quote is pullable).
- **Mobile-First:** Assume readers are on phones. Shorter paragraphs, clearer headings, minimal wall-of-text sections.
- **Medium-Specific:** If you're posting on Medium, Substack, LinkedIn, or your own blog, adapt to that platform's culture and format expectations.
