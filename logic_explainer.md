# Instructional Clarity & Pedagogy Module

*Pedagogy, scaffolding, and the "Explainer" document family.*

------

## 1. The "So What?" Factor

- **Context First:** Every chapter or major section must establish the "Why" (the value to the reader) before the "How" (the technical detail).
- **Front-Loading:** Place the most important information in the first paragraph of a section.

## 2. Concept Scaffolding

- **No "Magic" Terms:** Never use a domain-specific term or acronym before it has been defined or introduced in a "Quick Start" or "Definitions" section.
- **Cognitive Load:** Break complex multi-step processes into chunks of no more than 7 steps. Use sub-headings to provide "breathing room."

## 3. Practical Aids

- **The "For Example" Rule:** Abstract concepts (e.g., "The Law of Large Numbers" or "API Rate Limiting") must be followed by a concrete, relatable example.
- **Quick-Reference:** For documents over 5 pages, include a summary table, checklist, or "TL;DR" for high-frequency tasks.

## 4. Reader Agency

- **Peer Treatment:** Address the reader as a capable peer. Avoid condescension ("It is easy to see that...") or over-explanation of basic logic.
- **Addressing Counter-Arguments:** In explainers, proactively acknowledge common "Yeah, but..." objections the reader might have.
