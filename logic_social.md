# Social Media Writing Logic Module

*Platform norms, hooks, atomic clarity, and engagement signals.*

------

## 1. Platform Norms & Format

- **Know the Limits:** Twitter/X has a character limit; TikTok is video-first; LinkedIn favors professional insights; Instagram rewards visual + caption synergy. Adapt your message to the platform's constraints and culture.
- **Format for the Platform:** Use line breaks, emojis, hashtags, or threading according to platform norms. Don't cross-post identical text to all platforms without adaptation.
- **Timing Awareness:** Post when your audience is active. Understand each platform's algorithm preferences (threads get engagement, single posts decay fast on some platforms).

## 2. Hook & Opening

- **First Line is Everything:** On social media, if the opening line doesn't grab attention, the post is invisible. Lead with the most interesting or important part.
- **Pattern Interrupt:** Break the scroll. Use a surprising statement, a question, a bold claim, or a vivid image to stop thumb-scrollers in their tracks.
- **Promise Clarity:** Make clear in the opening what the post is about. Avoid cryptic or "you won't believe this" openers that disappoint.

## 3. Atomic Clarity

- **One Core Idea:** Each post should make one main point. If you're covering multiple angles, make separate posts or a thread.
- **No Jargon:** Assume your audience is scanning quickly and may not know your field. Use simple, clear language.
- **Scannable Structure:** Use short lines, line breaks, bullets, or numbering. Dense paragraphs get scrolled past.

## 4. Engagement & Tone

- **Direct Address:** Use "you," "I," "we" to create a sense of conversation. Avoid third-person institutional tone.
- **Authenticity Signals:** Share real perspectives, admit uncertainty, use conversational language. Polished corporate-speak reads as tone-deaf on social media.
- **Appropriate Humor:** Humor can increase engagement, but it must match your platform and audience. Know when dry wit works and when earnestness is better.

## 5. Call-to-Action or Engagement Prompt

- **Optional but Effective:** Questions, polls, and clear CTAs boost engagement. "What's your take?" or "Drop a comment" or "Link in bio" are common, effective patterns.
- **Keep it Brief:** A long CTA undermines the post. One sentence or a clear direction is enough.
- **Match the Action:** If you want comments, ask a real question (not "thoughts?"). If you want retweets, make a claim worth sharing.

## 6. Links & Threading

- **URL Preview Friendly:** If linking, use a clear headline so the preview is useful. Don't just dump a URL.
- **Threading for Depth:** If you need more than one post's worth of space, use threads (on Twitter/X) to keep related ideas together.
- **Minimal Links:** One link per post is often best. Multiple links can dilute engagement and read as spam.

## 7. Visual-Text Balance

- **Text Complements Visuals:** On image-heavy platforms (Instagram, TikTok), the caption should enhance the image, not stand alone. On text-first platforms (Twitter/X, LinkedIn), visuals are bonus.
- **Alt Text (When Applicable):** If posting images, include descriptive alt text for accessibility and SEO.
- **Consistency in Visuals:** If you use images, keep a consistent style or aesthetic to build brand recognition.

## 8. Tone Consistency Across Platforms

- **Same Voice, Different Tone:** Your core personality should carry across platforms, but adapt intensity to the platform's culture. More formal on LinkedIn, more casual on TikTok.
- **Avoid Whiplash:** Don't post something earnest on Twitter and sarcastic on Instagram. Readers who follow you across platforms will notice.
- **Platform-Appropriate Depth:** Twitter/X posts should be snappy; LinkedIn can handle more detail; TikTok is fast and visual. Match your depth to the medium.
